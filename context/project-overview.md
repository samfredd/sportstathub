Project Overview
Introduction
Sports betting is a multibillion‑dollar industry that attracts millions of fans worldwide. In Nigeria the market is particularly vibrant, with users relying on a mix of intuition, insider tips and fragmented statistics to make their selections. The goal of this project is to build a multi‑sport predictions hub that aggregates reliable data and provides transparent, data‑driven predictions for football, basketball, tennis and dozens of other bettable sports. Beyond mere predictions, the platform will include ready‑made booking codes, a community forum, advertising space for bookmakers, and a revenue‑sharing system for creators. The product is mobile‑first, supports dark mode, integrates local payment options and is designed for the Lagos, Nigeria timezone (Africa/Lagos).
Case Study: Insights from Statof
Statof.com is an advanced football statistics platform and serves as a valuable reference. Their mission is to make professional‑grade football data accessible to everyone, whether the user is a casual fan, content creator or analyst. Statof aggregates and presents football statistics across dozens of leagues and competitions, including goals, corners, cards, fouls, shots, offsides and possession. It tracks team form, head‑to‑head records, referee tendencies and historical trends to give users a full picture before kick‑off. The platform’s PRO Analysis engine processes thousands of data points per match to surface statistical insights, generating probability scores and confidence levels.
Statof’s platform operates in real time: it collects data from trusted sports providers and updates in near real time during matches. Its head‑to‑head engine compares any two teams across every metric, and the referee database stores card averages and foul tendencies for thousands of referees. Statof offers paid tiers—Pro and Pro Max—that unlock comprehensive statistics, unlimited head‑to‑head comparisons, rankings, referee profiles and the Smart Analyse feature. Proposed Platform Features
Multi‑Sport Predictions Hub – The platform will publish daily and weekly predictions across football, basketball, tennis, baseball, rugby, cricket, volleyball, handball and esports. Each prediction will include markets such as win/draw, over/under, handicaps, correct score, both‑teams‑to‑score and player props. Users can filter predictions by sport, league, date or market.
Booking Codes Section – A dedicated “Code Hub” will host ready‑made booking codes for all major Nigerian and international bookmakers (Bet9ja, Sportybet, 1xBet, BetKing, MSport, Betway, etc.). Each code entry will display the underlying slip and a one‑click “Copy Code” button with a deep link that opens the slip in the bookmaker’s app or website. Codes can be filtered by sport, date, odds range or creator.
Interactive Forum & Community Features – Comments sections will appear under every prediction and booking code. A forum with themed channels (e.g., “Today’s Matches Chat,” “Basketball Corner,” “Pundit Debate Room”) will allow users to discuss selections, argue statistics and share their own mini‑predictions. Registered users can follow threads, upvote posts and receive notifications when someone replies. Moderation tools and spam protection are built in.
Advertising Space for Betting Platforms – Banner slots, sponsored prediction modules and homepage takeovers will be available for bookmakers. An ad management module will rotate ads, track impressions/clicks and generate reports. Partnerships will comply with legal requirements and ensure that ads are clearly labelled.
Creator/Pundit Ecosystem – Users can create “Pundit” accounts to publish predictions and booking codes. Each creator has a profile page with follower counts, win rates and earnings. Revenue sharing is handled via affiliate links or code performance: the platform tracks how many users use a particular booking code and credits a percentage of the referral commission to the creator. Leaderboards showcase top creators by win rate, followers and revenue.
Thorough Sport‑Specific Analysis – Each match page will provide deep, sport‑specific analytics. For basketball this includes quarter‑by‑quarter scoring, player efficiency ratings, pace, offensive/defensive ratings and injury reports. Football pages will show expected goals (xG), form streaks, head‑to‑head results, set‑piece statistics and referee tendencies. Tennis pages will highlight head‑to‑head records, surface performance, serve/return percentages and break‑point conversion.
Game Stats Explorer (Self‑Prediction Tool) – A searchable database allows users to explore historical and live statistics. Filters include league, team, player, time period and stat category. Users can compare teams side‑by‑side across recent matches or generate charts to visualise trends, enabling self‑constructed predictions.
AI Predict – An AI recommendation engine analyses historical data, player and team form, head‑to‑head records, injuries and external factors (weather, home advantage) to suggest likely outcomes. Inspired by the PRO Analysis engine on Statof, the AI module will provide probability scores and confidence levels for each market. It will clearly state the data sources used, emphasise that predictions are suggestions rather than guarantees and warn users about responsible betting practices.
Mobile‑First Design and Dark Mode – Because most Nigerian users access betting sites via mobile devices, the interface will be designed with responsive layouts, minimal page weight and offline caching. A dark mode will reduce eye strain and support battery efficiency.
Notifications and Personalisation – Registered users can opt in to push notifications or in‑app alerts for new predictions, code updates, forum replies and favourite team statistics. Personalisation features will recommend content based on user preferences and history.
Target Audience and Market
The primary audience comprises sports fans and bettors in Nigeria and the wider African market. These users frequently use mobile devices, appreciate quick access to booking codes and rely on community insights. Secondary audiences include international bettors interested in African leagues and pundits seeking a platform to monetise their expertise. By offering multi‑sport coverage and self‑prediction tools, the platform appeals to both casual bettors and data‑driven analysts.
Monetisation and Revenue Model
Subscriptions – A freemium model similar to Statof can provide basic scores and limited predictions for free while charging for full statistics, AI predictions and advanced analytics. Tiers include Pro (complete statistics and unlimited code copying) and Pro Max (adds AI Predict and deeper insights).
Affiliate Commissions – Booking codes embed affiliate links to bookmakers. A share of every wager placed via these codes generates revenue. Creators earn a percentage of the commission generated by their codes.
Advertising – Sell banner impressions, sponsored slots and featured picks to bookmakers and sports brands. Ads are clearly identified and do not interfere with user experience.
Creator Subscriptions – Top pundits can offer premium tiers (e.g., VIP picks or private channels) to their followers, with the platform taking a transaction fee.
Unique Value Proposition
While Statof offers comprehensive football statistics, it focuses on a single sport and lacks community interaction, booking codes and creator monetisation. The proposed platform differentiates itself by:
Covering every bettable sport rather than just football.
Providing ready‑made booking codes for multiple bookmakers with one‑click copying.
Enabling community interaction through comments, forums and live chats.
Allowing creators to monetise their predictions and share in affiliate revenue.
Integrating AI‑powered predictions with transparent probability scores and clear explanations.
By combining comprehensive data, community engagement and flexible monetisation, the platform aims to become the leading destination for sports predictions in Nigeria and beyond.
Goals and Objectives
Launch an MVP in six months that supports football, basketball and tennis predictions, booking codes for major bookmakers and basic community features.
Expand coverage to 10+ sports within the first year, adding rugby, cricket, volleyball, handball and esports.
Achieve a user base of 50,000 active users in Nigeria within the first year through targeted marketing and partnerships.
Onboard at least 100 creators and establish a transparent revenue‑sharing scheme.
Integrate AI Predict by month 8, ensuring that models are trained on reliable data and evaluated for accuracy and fairness.
Implement dark mode, offline caching and push notifications to improve user retention and engagement.
These objectives provide concrete targets for development, marketing and product adoption.
